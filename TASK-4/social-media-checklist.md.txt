# Social Media Checklist for College Students

Using social media is a part of our daily life, but using it wisely really matters. Here are some simple do’s and don’ts that I try to follow as a college student:

## ✅ Do’s
- Think twice before posting anything, because once it’s online, it stays there.
- Be respectful while commenting or messaging others.
- Use privacy settings to keep your personal information safe.
- Share content that is meaningful, helpful, or creative.
- Give proper credit if you’re using someone else’s work or idea.
- Stay updated with current trends, but follow only what aligns with your values.
- Use social media for learning new skills and gaining knowledge.
- Maintain a positive and professional digital image.
- Double-check facts before sharing any news or information.
- Connect with people who inspire and motivate you.

## ❌ Don’ts
- Don’t share personal details like your phone number, address, or passwords.
- Avoid posting anything offensive, abusive, or inappropriate.
- Don’t get involved in unnecessary online arguments or fights.
- Never spread fake news or information without checking it.
- Don’t copy others’ content and present it as your own.
- Avoid wasting too much time scrolling without purpose.
- Don’t post when you’re angry or emotional—you might regret it later.
- Avoid comparing your life with others based on social media posts.
- Don’t click on suspicious links or unknown messages.
- Avoid oversharing your daily routine or live location.

---

**Note:** Being responsible on social media not only helps in building a positive image but also protects your privacy and future opportunities.
